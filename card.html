Killer Whale

Killer whales are highly social; some populations are composed of matrilineal family groups (pods) which are the most stable of any animal species.

  • Scientific Name: Orcinus orca
  • Average Length: 7 meters
  • Average Lifespan: 40 years
  • Habitat: Coastal oceans and seas

Large, black-and-white with a medium-sized white eye patch, killer whales live in open water. They reach 7 meters in length and live on average for 40 years. Killer whales get their name from mythologies of indigenous cultures, with their reputation ranging from being the souls of humans to merciless killers.
